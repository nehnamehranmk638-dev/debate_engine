Government regulation of AI systems would stifle innovation and put democratic nations at a competitive disadvantage. AI development moves at an extraordinary pace, and government bureaucracies are structurally incapable of keeping up. By the time regulations are drafted, debated, passed, and implemented, the technology they target will have evolved beyond recognition, leaving companies burdened with rules designed for yesterday's problems.

China and other authoritarian states are investing heavily in AI with no regulatory constraints. If Western democracies impose heavy regulatory burdens on their AI industries, they will cede AI leadership to countries with far worse human rights records and geopolitical intentions. The result would be a world where the most powerful AI systems are controlled by authoritarian governments, which is far more dangerous than the risks regulation aims to prevent.

The free market is better equipped than governments to regulate AI. Companies that build harmful AI systems face reputational damage, legal liability, and loss of customers. These market forces incentivize responsible development more effectively and efficiently than government mandates that apply one-size-fits-all rules to vastly different technologies and use cases.
