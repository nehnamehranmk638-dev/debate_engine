Government AI regulation raises serious concerns about free speech and the concentration of power in state hands. Governments that control which AI systems can operate also control what information those systems can generate and distribute. This creates a dangerous precedent where politicians and bureaucrats determine what AI can say, effectively giving governments a powerful tool for censorship and information control.

The economic costs of AI regulation would be enormous and fall disproportionately on consumers and small businesses. Compliance costs get passed down to end users, making AI-powered products more expensive and less accessible to people who need them most. In healthcare, education, and financial services, AI regulation could slow the deployment of tools that could save lives, improve learning outcomes, and expand access to financial services for underserved populations.

Most proposed AI regulations are solutions in search of problems. The harms that regulators cite are either already covered by existing laws — discrimination is illegal, fraud is illegal, privacy violations are covered by GDPR — or are speculative future risks that may never materialize. Creating an entirely new regulatory apparatus for AI is wasteful, redundant, and likely to do more harm than good by slowing beneficial applications without meaningfully reducing actual risks.
