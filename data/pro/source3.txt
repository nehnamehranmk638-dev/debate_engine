The argument that AI regulation stifles innovation is a myth perpetuated by companies that benefit from the absence of rules. In reality, clear regulatory frameworks provide certainty that actually encourages investment and innovation. Companies know what is allowed and what is not, reducing legal uncertainty and enabling long-term planning.

Regulation also builds public trust in AI systems. When citizens know that AI products have been tested, audited, and approved by independent bodies, they are more likely to adopt and benefit from these technologies. Without trust, even the most beneficial AI systems will face public resistance and rejection.

International coordination on AI regulation is already happening. The G7, the United Nations, and the OECD have all issued AI governance frameworks. Countries that refuse to regulate AI risk being excluded from international agreements and losing access to global markets that require compliance with AI safety standards.
